# NSLP Operating Guide — HR Follow-up, Verification & Implementation

## 1. What Is the NSLP

The New Skill Learning Program (NSLP) is an organisation-wide program in which staff learn new skills and are required to apply them in daily work with measurable, sustained improvement.

**The NSLP is not completed when the meeting ends.** It is completed only when:
1. The learned skill is applied in daily work.
2. Measurable improvement is proven (Before vs After evidence).
3. Improvement is sustained over time.

HR's role is follow-up, verification, evidence collection, adoption tracking, and management reporting — not teaching or creating skill content.

---

## 2. HR Role Summary

| Responsibility | Detail |
|---|---|
| Skill ID confirmation | Every new skill must be assigned a unique Skill ID before the session |
| BGCT document assurance | Every new skill must have a dedicated BGCT document |
| Before & After evidence | Collect and validate evidence that the skill was applied and improvement occurred |
| Team participation | Ensure all relevant teams participate and contribute |
| Adoption tracking | Track adoption, implementation, and business impact across departments |
| Career confirmation | Confirm NSLP contributes to career development with measurable outcomes |
| Management reporting | Report implementation status, adoption rate, and business impact with evidence |

---

## 3. Process Flow

```
New Skill Meeting
       ↓
Skill Application in Work
       ↓
Measurable Improvement
       ↓
HR Verification
       ↓
SUCCESS STORY / Escalation
```

Each stage has HR responsibilities. No stage may be skipped.

---

## 4. Before the Meeting

| # | HR Responsibility |
|---|---|
| 1 | Confirm the selected skill qualifies as a new skill (not a repeat of existing knowledge) |
| 2 | Confirm the skill is relevant and valuable to the business |
| 3 | Send the New Skill Learning Guideline to all participants |
| 4 | Record attendees in the Skill Learning Register |
| 5 | Ensure participants understand they must apply the skill after the meeting |
| 6 | Add attendees to the Skill Learning Register with session date |
| 7 | Reject the session if the skill is not new — escalate to management |

**Gate check:** If the skill is not new, the session must not proceed. HR raises this before the meeting, not after.

---

## 5. During the Meeting

| # | HR Responsibility |
|---|---|
| 1 | Ensure the trainer/host teaches a completely new skill |
| 2 | Ensure participants are actively involved |
| 3 | Ensure real job examples are shown (not theory only) |
| 4 | Ensure every participant defines how they will use the skill in daily work |
| 5 | Ensure every participant submits an Action Plan Card before leaving |

**Gate check:** No participant leaves without submitting an Action Plan Card.

---

## 6. After the Meeting

| # | HR Responsibility |
|---|---|
| 1 | Start the 2-week countdown for each participant |
| 2 | Remind participants to apply the skill in daily work |
| 3 | Collect improvement evidence after 2 weeks |

**Timer:** The countdown starts on the day of the meeting. The 2-week evaluation date is recorded in the register.

---

## 7. 2-Week Evaluation

| # | HR Responsibility |
|---|---|
| 1 | Ask whether the skill is being applied continuously |
| 2 | Ask what difference the skill made |
| 3 | Ask for measurable proof |
| 4 | Compare Before evidence vs After evidence |
| 5 | Check whether improvement is sustained (not just 1–2 days) |

**Outcome:** Assign one of the four outcome labels (see §9).

---

## 8. Daily, Weekly, and Monthly Operating Rhythm

### Daily

- Check new skill sessions scheduled or completed.
- Check whether every new skill has a Skill ID.
- Check whether each skill has a BGCT document.
- Check whether Action Plan Cards are received from the latest session.
- Follow up on pending Before & After evidence.
- Track participants who have reached the 2-week evaluation point.

### Weekly

- Review the Skill Learning Register.
- Review adoption and implementation status across teams.
- Check all pending 2-week evaluations.
- Validate submitted Before & After evidence.
- Identify exceptions and route or escalate where required.
- Update management-ready status summary.

### Monthly

- Prepare NSLP implementation status report.
- Report adoption rate, implementation rate, and business impact.
- Identify high-impact skills and weak or no-impact skills.
- Review whether improvements are sustained.
- Recommend continuation, redesign, reskill, or stop decisions to management.

---

## 9. Outcome Labels

| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **SUCCESS STORY** | Skill applied and sustained improvement proven |
| **NOT EFFECTIVE** | Skill applied but no improvement shown |
| **FAILURE** | Skill not applied after meeting |
| **UNSUSTAINED** | Improvement happened then dropped |

Each participant at each 2-week evaluation receives one outcome label. The label is recorded in the register and referenced in the management report.

## 11. Documents Maintained by HR

| Document | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Skill Learning Register | Master log of all NSLP sessions and participants |
| Skill ID Register | Unique IDs assigned to each new skill |
| BGCT Document Register | Tracks which skills have BGCT documents |
| Action Plan Card Register | Tracks card submission per participant |
| Before & After Evidence Register | Evidence collection log |
| 2-Week Evaluation Log | One entry per participant per session |
| Adoption & Implementation Tracker | Cross-team status view |
| NSLP Exception Register | Logs all 7 exception types |
| Success Story Database | Confirmed SUCCESS STORY cases |
| NSLP Management Report | Monthly report to management |

---

## 12. Boundaries — What HR Does Not Do

| Boundary |
|---|
| HR does not approve old skills as NSLP |
| HR does not mark attendance as success without implementation evidence |
| HR does not accept opinion-only proof |
| HR does not mark NSLP complete without measurable Before & After improvement |
| HR does not create technical skill content unless separately assigned |
| HR does not replace management decision-making — HR verifies and reports evidence |

---

## 13. Pass/Fail Rule

**PASS if:**
- Every new skill has a Skill ID
- Every new skill has a BGCT document
- Participants submit Action Plan Cards
- Before & After evidence exists
- Improvement is measurable
- Improvement is sustained
- HR records adoption, implementation, and business impact
- Management receives an evidence-backed status report

**FAIL if:**
- No Skill ID exists
- No BGCT document exists
- No Action Plan Card exists
- No Before & After evidence exists
- Improvement is opinion-only
- Skill is not applied
- Improvement is not sustained
- Management report has no evidence
