# NEW SKILL LEARNING PROGRAM — HR Follow-up, Verification & Implementation Responsibility

## Overview

The New Skill Learning Program is completed only when the learned skill is applied in daily work, measurable improvement is proven, and improvement is sustained over time. HR is responsible for follow-up, verification, evidence collection, adoption tracking, and management reporting.

---

## My Role

- Confirming that every new skill is assigned a unique Skill ID.
- Ensuring that each new skill has a dedicated BGCT (Business Guideline & Company Training) document.
- Verifying that employees have implemented the newly learned skill by collecting and validating Before & After implementation evidence.
- Ensuring that all relevant teams actively participate in and contribute to the New Skill Learning Program.
- Tracking the adoption, implementation, and business impact of each new skill across departments.
- Confirming that the New Skill Learning Program contributes to employees' career development, enhances their professional capabilities, and improves their job performance through measurable outcomes.
- Reporting the overall implementation status, adoption rate, and business impact of the New Skill Learning Program to management with supporting evidence.

---

## Process Flow

New Skill Meeting → Skill Application in Work → Measurable Improvement → HR Verification → Success Story / Escalation

NSLP is not completed when the meeting ends. NSLP is completed only when the staff member applies the skill and proves improvement.

---

## HR Responsibilities by Stage

### Before the Meeting

- Confirm the selected skill qualifies as a new skill.
- Confirm the skill is relevant and valuable to the business.
- Send the New Skill Learning Guideline to participants.
- Record attendees.
- Ensure participants understand they must apply the skill after the meeting.
- Add attendees to the Skill Learning Register.
- Reject the session if the skill is not new and escalate to management.

### During / Meeting Governance

- Ensure the trainer/host teaches a completely new skill.
- Ensure participants are actively involved.
- Ensure real job examples are shown.
- Ensure participants define how they will use the skill in daily work.
- Ensure every participant submits an Action Plan Card before leaving.

### After the Meeting

- Start the 2-week countdown for each participant.
- Remind participants to apply the skill in daily work.
- Collect improvement evidence after 2 weeks.

### 2-Week Evaluation

- Ask whether the skill is being applied continuously.
- Ask what difference it made.
- Ask for measurable proof.
- Compare Before vs After evidence.
- Check whether the improvement is sustained.

---

## Evidence Required

- Skill ID
- BGCT document link/reference
- Attendee list
- Action Plan Card
- Before evidence
- After evidence
- KPI / time saved / output / quality / sales / complaint reduction proof where applicable
- Adoption status
- Implementation status
- Business impact summary
- HR verification result
- Management report reference

---

## Outcome Labels

- **SUCCESS STORY** — skill applied and sustained improvement proven
- **NOT EFFECTIVE** — skill applied but no improvement
- **FAILURE** — skill not applied after meeting
- **UNSUSTAINED** — improvement happened then dropped

---

## Exception Monitoring

- Skill not applied
- Skill applied but no improvement
- Improvement only for 1–2 days
- No proof provided
- Staff claims improvement but numbers do not change
- Senior staff repeats old skill as new skill
- Trainer gives theory instead of applicable skill

---

## Daily Activities

- Check new skill sessions scheduled or completed.
- Check whether every new skill has a Skill ID.
- Check whether each skill has a BGCT document.
- Check whether Action Plan Cards are received.
- Follow up on pending Before & After evidence.
- Track participants who have reached the 2-week evaluation point.

---

## Weekly Activities

- Review Skill Learning Register.
- Review adoption and implementation status across teams.
- Check pending 2-week evaluations.
- Validate submitted Before & After evidence.
- Identify exceptions and route/escalate where required.
- Update management-ready status summary.

---

## Monthly Activities

- Prepare NSLP implementation status report.
- Report adoption rate, implementation rate, and business impact.
- Identify high-impact skills and weak/no-impact skills.
- Review whether improvements are sustained.
- Recommend continuation, redesign, reskill, or stop decisions to management.

---

- 2-Week Evaluation Log

- NSLP Exception Register
- Success Story Database

## KPIs / Success Measures

- % of new skills with Skill ID
- % of new skills with BGCT document
- Action Plan Card submission rate
- Before & After evidence completion rate
- 2-week evaluation completion rate
- Skill adoption rate
- Skill implementation rate
- Sustained improvement rate
- Success Story count
- Exception count
- Business impact reported to management

---

## Boundaries / What HR Does Not Do

- HR does not approve old skills as NSLP.
- HR does not mark attendance as success without implementation evidence.
- HR does not accept opinion-only proof.
- HR does not mark NSLP complete without measurable Before & After improvement.
- HR does not create technical skill content unless assigned separately.
- HR does not replace management decision-making; HR verifies and reports evidence.

---

## Pass/Fail Rule

**PASS if:**
- Every new skill has a Skill ID.
- Every new skill has a BGCT document.
- Participants submit Action Plan Cards.
- Before & After evidence exists.
- Improvement is measurable.
- Improvement is sustained.
- HR records adoption, implementation, and business impact.
- Management receives an evidence-backed status report.

**FAIL if:**
- No Skill ID exists.
- No BGCT document exists.
- No Action Plan Card exists.
- No Before & After evidence exists.
- Improvement is opinion-only.
- Skill is not applied.
- Improvement is not sustained.
- Management report has no evidence.
