# **Recruitment Quality Control Process**

**Candidate Pipeline**  
Every candidate enters the recruitment pipeline through internal and external referrals, social media advertisements, recruitment websites, and other sourcing channels. Candidates are shortlisted based on position requirements and vacancy availability.

All candidates are assessed against our 8-point screening criteria before progressing to the interview stage:  
• O/L Mathematics  
• English Proficiency  
• IT Skills  
• E-Commerce Knowledge  
• AI Familiarity  
• Salary Alignment  
• Availability  
• Commitment

**Interview Records**  
During the final interview (online or in-person), candidates are evaluated across five key areas:  
• Technical Knowledge  
• AI Knowledge  
• Communication Skills  
• Confidence  
• Cultural Fit

Each area is scored out of 10 marks, for a total of 50 marks. Candidates who score above 30 marks may be considered for recruitment, subject to meeting company requirements and agreeing to the offered salary and employment terms.

Separate tracking sheets are maintained for:  
• Rejected Candidates  
• On-Hold Candidates

These records support future recruitment requirements and talent pooling.

**Recruitment Commitments**  
Every offer includes a documented commitment record outlining what the candidate has agreed to deliver during the interview and after the initial 7-day evaluation period.

A review is scheduled after the first 7/14 days, incorporating feedback from the Trainer / Team Leader evaluation reports, to assess:  
• Knowledge gained during training  
• Understanding of assigned responsibilities  
• Confidence level and communication skills  
• AI/LLM adoption and practical application  
• Ability to perform assigned tasks independently  
• Commitment to agreed objectives  
• Areas requiring further support or improvement

All commitments are linked to measurable review checkpoints at Months 1, 3, and 6 to ensure performance assessments remain evidence-based.

**Month 1 Review**  
Assessment Areas:  
• Onboarding completion  
• System and tool access  
• Initial assignments  
• Early commitment verification

Employee Status Categories:  
• On Track  
• Watch  
• Concern  
• Critical

Any employee marked as “Concern” during the Month 1 review will receive a documented corrective action plan with clear responsibilities and deadlines before the Month 3 review.

**Month 3 Review**  
Assessment Areas:  
• Commitment delivery review  
• KPI participation  
• Behavioural observations  
• Corrective action progress

Employees who are marked as "Concern" during the Month 1 review and fail to achieve the agreed corrective action plan by the Month 3 review may be considered for discontinuation during their probation period, subject to management review and approval.

**Month 6 Review**  
Assessment Areas:  
• Full commitment versus delivery assessment  
• Probation confirmation decision  
• Formal handover preparation

**Source Quality Monitoring**  
All recruitment sources are monitored across two dimensions:

**Activity Metrics**  
• Applications received  
• Response rates  
• Recruitment costs

**Quality Metrics**  
• Long-term hiring success  
• Probation completion rates  
• Performance outcomes

**Handover After 180 Days**  
At Month 6, a structured 15-minute handover meeting will be conducted involving the Line Manager, Mayoorika, Arun, and myself.

The review will cover:  
• Commitment delivery summary  
• Employee record verification  
• KPI baseline confirmation  
• Outstanding issues and assigned owners

Recruitment ownership formally concludes at this stage unless unresolved actions remain.

**Daily Knowledge Capture**  
Effective immediately, I will dedicate one hour each working day to documenting:

• Evaluation and training status  
• Recruitment trends  
• Sourcing insights  
• Interview observations  
• Probation concerns  
• Commitment gaps

This process will ensure continuous improvement in recruitment quality, onboarding effectiveness, and long-term employee success.
