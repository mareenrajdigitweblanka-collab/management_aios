## **DIGIT WEB LANKA - Company Policy Manual**

## **1.0 Introduction**

## **1.1 Purpose and Reasoning**

This policy manual serves as the foundation for how DIGIT WEB LANKA (PVT) LTD conducts its business operations and interacts with its employees. Our goal is to establish clear expectations, create a positive work environment, and ensure alignment with legal and ethical standards. These policies aim to support a productive, professional, and respectful workplace, helping both the company and its employees achieve their full potential.

By setting out these policies, we seek to:

- Provide clarity on company rules and procedures to avoid misunderstandings.

- Maintain consistency in decision-making and operations.

- Protect the rights of both the company and its employees.

- Foster a culture of accountability and transparency.

## **1.2 Scope**

The policies outlined in this manual apply to all employees of DIGIT WEB LANKA (PVT) LTD, including full-time, part-time, and contract staff. All employees are required to read, understand, and adhere to these policies as a condition of their employment.

## **2.0 Salary Confidentiality Policy**

To maintain a fair and respectful workplace, DIGIT WEB LANKA (PVT) LTD enforces a strict salary confidentiality policy. Employees are not permitted to share or discuss their individual salaries, bonuses, or allowances with other employees. This policy helps prevent feelings of inequity or dissatisfaction that can arise from misunderstandings or miscommunication about compensation.

## **2.1 Termination of Agreement**

During the evaluation or probationary period, either the employee or the company may terminate the employment agreement. The terms and conditions for termination must be followed to ensure a smooth transition.

## **2.2 Payment upon Vacation or Termination**

- **Voluntary Resignation:** No additional payment will be provided

- **Company-Initiated Termination:** Payment will be provided based on the number of days worked.

- Employees must familiarise themselves with these policies and adhere to them during their employment.

## **3.0 Onboarding Policy**

DIGIT WEB LANKA places great emphasis on onboarding new employees effectively. The onboarding policy is designed to help new hires integrate into the company, understand their roles and company culture, and become part of the team as quickly as possible. A structured onboarding process improves employee engagement, retention, and satisfaction.

- **Pre-Employment Requirements:** Collection of all necessary documents, such as ID, signed contracts, and background checks, before the start date to ensure compliance.

- **Orientation and Introduction:** A detailed orientation on the first day that includes an introduction to the team, overview of company culture, and an explanation of key policies such as confidentiality and work schedules.

- **Training & Support:** New employees receive role-specific training and are paired with a mentor or supervisor to help them navigate their new responsibilities.

- **Probation and Feedback:** A clear probation period is defined, during which employees receive regular feedback to track their progress and fit within the organization.

## **4.0 No-Gossip Policy**

DIGIT WEB LANKA (PVT) LTD believes in maintaining a positive and respectful workplace. Gossip and negative comments can undermine teamwork, create a toxic work environment, and harm professional relationships. Therefore, the company enforces a no-gossip policy, which includes:

## ● **Prohibited Behaviors:**

- Discussing someone’s personal or professional life without their consent.

- Making negative or disparaging comments about other employees or management.

○ Sharing or repeating unverified rumors.

Employees found violating this policy may face disciplinary actions, including termination of employment.

## **4.1 Constructive Communication and Feedback**

## **Professionalism in Communication:**

All employees are expected to maintain professional conduct in their interactions with colleagues, management, and stakeholders. Negative or unconstructive, personalized comments about staff members, team leaders, management, or company procedures are not permitted in the workplace. This includes making off-the-cuff remarks or unconstructively criticizing others or company procedures in an open space or in the presence of other staff members.

## **Appropriate Channels for Feedback:**

If an employee has concerns or feedback regarding colleagues, management decisions, procedures, or any company policy, they should use the following appropriate channels:

- **One-on-One Discussions:** Engage in private discussions with the concerned party, immediate supervisor, or manager.

- **Written Feedback to the Relevant Department or Supervisor:** Employees may provide written feedback about any concerns to the respective department head or supervisor.

- **Critique Meetings:** Raise issues or concerns during scheduled critique meetings where constructive feedback is encouraged.

- **Written Feedback to the Company Director:** Employees can directly submit written feedback to the Company Director if they believe their concerns need to be addressed at the highest level or if other channels have not resolved the issue satisfactorily.

## **Constructive Criticism:**

When providing feedback, employees should focus on constructive criticism that offers solutions or suggestions for improvement. All feedback should be specific, actionable, and related to work performance or procedures.

## **Confidentiality:**

Discussions about sensitive work-related matters should be conducted in private settings to maintain confidentiality and avoid any discomfort or misunderstandings among employees.

## **Escalation Process:**

If an employee feels their concerns are not adequately addressed through the above channels, they should follow the company's established escalation process to bring the matter to higher-level management for resolution.

## **Consequences:**

Violations of this policy, including making unconstructive or off-the-cuff remarks about other staff, leaders, managers, or company procedures in an open space or in front of other employees, may result in disciplinary action as outlined in the employee handbook. This policy is in place to maintain a respectful and positive work environment.

## **Positive Work Culture:**

Employees are encouraged to contribute to a positive work culture by focusing on solutions, collaboration, and mutual respect.

## **5.0 Late Arrival Policy**

## **Purpose and Importance:**

Punctuality is essential for the smooth operation and overall productivity of DIGIT WEB LANKA. When employees arrive on time, it helps ensure that meetings, project timelines, and team collaboration are not disrupted. It sets a professional standard and demonstrates commitment and reliability, both to the organization and fellow colleagues.

To maintain this standard, the Late Arrival Policy outlines clear expectations regarding attendance and the consequences for arriving late. This policy encourages employees to be punctual, fosters a culture of responsibility, and ensures that time management is respected across all levels of the organization.

## **Allowable Tardiness:**

Employees are allowed a maximum of **two instances of late arrival** per month, during which only the exact amount of time lost will have to be covered. This provides some flexibility for occasional, unforeseen circumstances (e.g., traffic delays or minor emergencies) without imposing immediate harsh penalties.

## **Consequences:**

For any late arrivals beyond the allowable two instances, the following deductions and consequences will be applied:

- **15 minutes late:** 30 minutes of pay deduction.

- **15-30 minutes late:** 2 hours of pay deduction.

- **30-60 minutes late:** 4 hours of pay deduction.

- **More than 60 minutes late:** 4 hours of pay deduction or more, based on the severity and frequency.

Repeated tardiness beyond the allowable two instances can escalate to more severe consequences, such as:

- **Frequent Late Arrivals:** If an employee is late more than two times in a month or develops a pattern of frequent tardiness, the level of pay deductions will increase incrementally. This serves as a warning to emphasize the importance of punctuality.

- **Disciplinary Action:** Consistent late arrivals may lead to formal disciplinary action, including verbal or written warnings, suspension, or potential termination of employment. The company views habitual lateness as a lack of respect for company policies and a disruption to team productivity.

## **6.0 Leave Policy**

## **6.1 General Leave Guidelines**

- Advance Notice: Leave requests must be updated in the leave system at least 48 hours in advance.

- Team Leave Limits: Maximum of two team members (or one in select teams: CST/Content writing/Postage) may take leave on any given day.

- Leave Planning: Employees should plan ahead to increase approval chances.

- Leave Limit: A maximum of 12% of staff can take leave simultaneously. Any excess will be considered unauthorised leave.

- Medical/Sick Leave: Only hospitalised medical conditions supported by reports will be considered for sick leave.

- Leave Plan Information:

  - 3 days: 1-week notice. Subteam leader notice

  - 4-7 days: 4-week notice. Team leader notice

  - 7-12 days: 8-week notice and HR approval.

  - 13-14 days: 3-month notice with Manager approval.

## **6.2 Planned Leave**

- Leave During Probation: No leave during the 3-month probationary period. If urgent, leave can be taken and recovered within the weekdays.

- For Juniors, there will be one day leave per month, and for seniors, there will be 2 days leave per month (32 days leave per annum).

- Please note that leave coverage will not be available on Saturdays.

## **6.3 Unplanned Leave**

- Leave Entitlement: 5 unplanned leave days per year.

- Notification: Notify HR or supervisors as soon as possible.

- Consequences: Excess unplanned leave is considered unauthorised and may lead to disciplinary action.

## **6.4 Short Leave and Early-Off Policy**

- Limits: Maximum 2 hours per month.

- Approval: Requires 24-hour advance notice and supervisor approval.

## **6.5 Maternity Leave Policy**

- Duration: 3 months for the 1st or 2nd baby.

- Notice: 30 days prior notice is required.

- Full Pay: Full-time employees receive full salary during leave.

## **7.0 Office Phone Usage Policy**

## **Purpose and Importance:**

Mobile phones can significantly hinder productivity and focus in the workplace. We strive to create a distraction-free environment that promotes concentration, efficiency, and professionalism. This Office Phone Usage Policy ensures employees remain focused on their tasks while providing reasonable access to phones for genuine needs or emergencies.

## **Policy Guidelines:**

## 1. **No Phone Usage Inside the Office Premises:**

- Employees are not allowed to use their phones within the main office premises during working hours. This includes making calls, texting, browsing the internet, or any other form of phone use.

- If phone usage is necessary (e.g., for a family emergency or urgent personal matter), the employee should **step outside the working area** and move to a designated break or outdoor area to use their phone discreetly. This helps maintain focus within the office and prevents distractions to other employees.

## 2. **Emergency Use:**

- Phones may be used in emergency situations only. In such cases, employees are permitted to make brief calls (up to 5 minutes). Emergencies may include urgent

personal matters such as family health issues, accidents, or other unexpected events.

- If an emergency call is expected, employees should inform their supervisor or manager in advance so that reasonable accommodations can be made.

## 3. **Social Media, Messaging, and Non-Work Related Usage:**

- Accessing social media platforms (e.g., Facebook, Instagram, Twitter), messaging apps (e.g., WhatsApp), or any other non-work-related activities is strictly prohibited during work hours.

- These activities should be confined to designated break times (e.g., lunch or tea breaks) and should be done outside the main office space to avoid disrupting the work environment.

## 4. **Phone Settings:**

- Phones must remain on silent mode throughout the workday. This prevents disruptive ring tones or alerts from interrupting the workflow and concentration of other employees.

## 5. **Consequences for Violations:**

- Any violation of this policy, including unauthorized phone usage inside the office, accessing social media during work hours, or creating distractions for colleagues, will result in disciplinary action.

- Disciplinary measures may include verbal or written warnings, temporary suspension from duties, or, in repeated cases, termination of employment.

## 6. **Reasonable Accommodations:**

- DIGIT WEB LANKA recognizes that there may be exceptional circumstances where employees need to be accessible by phone. In such cases, employees should discuss their needs with their manager, who may approve reasonable accommodations on a temporary basis (e.g., allowing phone use during a specific period).

## **Management Perspective and Considerations:**

The aim of this policy is not to restrict employees unnecessarily but to encourage them to develop healthy work habits and minimize distractions that can affect overall team productivity. By creating a designated space and clear boundaries for phone usage, the company ensures that employees can focus during work hours while still having access to their devices when truly necessary.

## **9.0 Focus Time Policy**

While we value teamwork and the benefits of collaboration, most tasks at DIGIT WEB LANKA can be carried out independently. We encourage employees to perform their duties without unnecessarily distracting colleagues. **Focus Time** is a designated period during which employees are expected to work on their primary tasks without interruptions. It is a structured approach to minimize distractions from discussions, meetings, emails, and other activities, allowing employees to fully immerse themselves in their work.

## **Focus Time Schedules:**

There are two distinct schedules based on different start times and work hours (Friday working time).

## **8:30 AM Start Schedule: (Mon-Thurs)**

## ● **8:30 AM – 10:30 AM:**

The first uninterrupted two-hour work session of the day. This block is considered a “sacred time” for deep work, where employees can focus on their most critical tasks.During this time slot, Skype communication is restricted. However, exceptions are made for conversations within the Image Editing Group and the Warehouse Group.

## ● **10:30 AM – 10:50 AM:**

A 20-minute break to allow for a standup meeting, brief check-ins, or any necessary discussions. This is the time to address questions or quick updates that do not require extensive time.

## ● **10:50 AM – 12:45 PM:**

Another solid stretch of uninterrupted work time, providing an opportunity to dive deep into projects or tasks without external interruptions. Employees should focus on progressing through their planned activities or tackling complex problems that require sustained attention.

## ● **2:00 PM – 3:00 PM:**

The afternoon focus hour, ensuring employees can finish strong before the end of the day. This session is designed to wrap up ongoing tasks or start planning for the next day’s priorities.

## **9:00 AM Start Schedule:**

## ● **9:00 AM – 11:00 AM:**

The first two-hour session of deep work. This block is intended to set the tone for the day, allowing employees to start with clear objectives and undisturbed focus.

## ● **11:00 AM – 11:10 AM:**

- A short 10-minute break for brief meetings, discussions, or a quick pause to refresh before resuming focused work.

- **11:10 AM – 12:45 PM:**

The second uninterrupted work block where employees continue to concentrate on their tasks, progressing steadily through their priorities.

## **10.0 Offboarding Policy**

At DIGIT WEB LANKA, we strive to ensure that employee transitions are managed smoothly and professionally. The following outlines the steps and procedures for offboarding employees, whether through resignation or termination.

## **10.1 Notice Period for Resignation**

Employees are required to provide a two-month notice (60 days) before resigning. This allows the company to prepare for the departure and ensure a smooth handover of duties.

## **10.2 Immediate Resignation**

Employees who resign on short notice will not be entitled to any unpaid wages or company benefits. This ensures minimal disruption to company operations.

## **10.3 Termination by the Company**

The company reserves the right to terminate employment under certain circumstances. Typically, the company will provide a two-month notice period for termination. However, in cases of gross misconduct or serious breaches of company policy, immediate termination may be issued without notice.

Gross misconduct includes, but is not limited to:

- Theft

- Harassment

- Fraud

- Breach of confidentiality

- Any behavior that significantly disrupts the workplace or jeopardizes the company’s reputation and operations

In these situations, the company will take prompt action to safeguard its interests and maintain a safe, professional, and ethical work environment.

## **10.4 Return of Company Property**

Employees must return all company property, including account details, devices, and any other materials issued during their employment, prior to leaving the company.

## **10.5 Revocation of Critical Credentials**

Upon resignation or termination, all critical login credentials will be promptly revoked to ensure the security of company systems and data.

## **10.6 Exit Interview**

An exit interview will be conducted by HR to confirm that all company property, digital assets, and duties have been returned or transferred appropriately.

## **10.7 Final Checklist**

A comprehensive offboarding checklist will be completed and signed by the employee, their manager, and an HR representative. This ensures all procedures are followed, and responsibilities are completed before the employee's departure.

## **10.8 Post-Departure Follow-Up**

After the employee's departure, the following actions will be taken:

- Review to ensure all digital assets have been transferred and accounted for.

- Monitor for any unauthorized access attempts to company systems.

- Employees are prohibited from accessing or using any company digital assets after their termination or resignation.

- Send a reminder to the former employee about their ongoing confidentiality obligations. Failure to comply with this policy may result in legal action.

## **17.0 Mandatory AI Tools Policy – AI is Not Optional**

Policy Declaration

higher-value contributions. It is not a suggestion—it is an operational standard.

1. Sub team-Specific AI Toolkits

1. KPI Measurement & Team-Level Impact

- AI contribution will be measured at both:

- Individual KPI level – output, efficiency, and skill growth.

- Sub-team level – overall workflow enhancements and strategic gains.

1. Monitoring & Enforcement

- AI usage will be reviewed during weekly performance reviews.

- Non-compliance will follow this escalation:

1. Formal warning

2. Performance impact

3. Escalation to HR with potential removal from key responsibilities

4. Policy Evolution

- This policy will be reviewed quarterly.

- Employees will be notified of:

- Tool updates

- Policy changes

- New mandatory tools or training requirements

All employees must:

- Integrate AI into their daily workflow
